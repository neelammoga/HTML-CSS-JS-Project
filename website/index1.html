What do we offer in our basic report?

Our basic car history check is great for shortlisting your vehicle's information and making a safe choice. Not sure about going for a full

check? Please go for a basic check and ensure the car you hope to buy ticks off the all-important checks. You could save some money,

plus it will act as a guide and help you to shortlist your next car.

  • Number of Previous Owners:

    You'll be given full details on total number of previous owners as recorded by the DVLA, which includes the date of registration. The higher the number of total previous owners, the lower the value it will likely fetch. Our report will not provide the previous owners' vital information; such as contact or personal details, but you can request information about the vehicle's registered owners from the DVLA should you need to.

  • Number Plate Change History:

    Check if the vehicle's number plate has any change history, including the date of plate change and the VRM it was changed to.

  • Scrap status:

    Check whether the car history you want to know has been scrapped or not. We will source data from the DVLA.

  • V5C:

    To ensure that a vehicle registration certificate (V5C) was not issued for a car listed as stolen or cloned, taking the identity of a destroyed or scrapped vehicle.

  • VIN or Chassis:

    Check the VIN of the vehicle and make sure the details you've been given match the information held by the DVLA. It's a crucial check to make sure the car is legitimate.

  • Engine Number:

    • Check that the engine number matches the vehicle engine number recorded by the DVLA

What checks are included in the full report?

Our full vehicle history report is the most comprehensive and affordable car check service in the UK. It will help you to make the right

decision before buying your next used car.

Our full vehicle check provides everything from basic checks, important checks and critical checks, which include:

  • Outstanding Finance Check:

    This check includes finance agreement details and provides a finance company agreement number
    (where available). Buying a car with outstanding finance could be very risky. Please read our guide to see how to avoid buying a car
    with outstanding finance. Use 'askMID' to check if your car is insured.

  • Insurance Write-off (complete loss):

    We check whether the vehicle has been declared a total loss or written-off, including the loss
    type and loss date. Insurance companies write-off cars when the cost of repairs is too high compared to the value of the vehicle.

  • Police Stolen Check:

    Verify whether the vehicle you are interested in is recorded as stolen in the Police National Computer and don't
    be a victim of vehicle fraud.

  • Certificate of Destruction:

    Check whether the vehicle has been issued a Certificate of Destruction, aka COD for short. It's a legal
    document issued by the DVLA when the vehicle is scrapped by an Authorized Treatment Facility (ATF) that has destroyed and
    recycled a vehicle.

  • High risk check:

    Check if the vehicle has been flagged as high risk. It is often related to the financial dispute over ownership and
    usually means the third party tracks it to prevent it from being sold. See our sample full reg check report for more details.

  • Mileage anomaly:

    To check whether the car is displaying the genuine mileage, our report shows if there is an inconsistency with the
    mileage reading held in premium mileage history sources. Our free car check includes mileage and clocking reports from the
    mileage recorded when the vehicle was MOT tested.
